Take five!

A stand-alone, semantically-consistent, microdata-oriented, pure-CSS slide renderer.

Manual

Introduction

Take five! is a slide renderer written in pure CSS (no JavaScript required), orientated to the semantics of HTML (i.e., semantic nodes and “HTML5 Microdata”). Before further reading, please consider to see the live demo.

Getting started

For creating a slide with Take five!, all you need to do is to include takefive.css in your document, and then use one of the following basic syntaxes:

  • <article id="my_hash1" itemscope class="slide">
        <element-to-be-slided />
    </article>
  • <article id="my_hash2" itemscope class="slide">
        <nav>
            <a href="#nowhere" rel="up"></a>
        </nav>
        <element-to-be-slided />
    </article>
  • <article id="my_hash3" itemscope class="slide">
        <element-to-be-slided />
        <nav>
            <a href="#nowhere" rel="up"></a>
        </nav>
    </article>
  • <article id="my_hash4" itemscope class="slide">
        <nav>
            <a href="#nowhere" rel="up">My home</a>
            <a href="#my_hash2" rel="prev">[previous slide name]</a>
            <a href="#my_hash2" rel="next">[next slide name]</a>
        </nav>
        <header>
            <h1>[title]</h1>
        </header>
        <this-element-will-be-slided />
        <this-element-will-be-displayed-on-the-right-side-of-the-screen />
        <this-element-will-be-displayed-on-the-right-side-of-the-screen />
        <etc. />
        <footer>[footer]</footer>
        <aside>[aside]</aside>
    </article>

Example

The following minimalistic code

    <p style="text-align: center; font-size: 2em;"><a href="#photo_of_the_day">Photo of the day</a></p>

    <article class="slide" id="photo_of_the_day">
        <nav><a href="#nowhere" rel="up"></a></nav>
        <img src="images/1.jpg" alt="Photo of the day" />
    </article>

will produce this result.

Slides containers (viewports)

Each slide is defined through the element <article id="my-hash-tag" class="slide"> … </article>. Such element is constructed in a way that:

  • it is independent and does not require any special container; it can nevertheless link to other slides, forming so a gallery
  • it can contain any kind of children elements and in any number (but be aware of the limits of the screen)
  • no further classes are required by the CSS, beside the "slide" class defined above.

Several slides belonging to a single gallery can be logically packed together within any parent element – normally a <section> element is chosen for this.

Each slide is displayed fixed, fullscreen and can be accessed through its hashtag.

Children elements

  • Direct children of the viewport:
    1. The first <nav> element found to be direct child of the slide will be placed on the left side of the viewport. In order to avoid confusion, further <nav> elements found to be direct children of the slide will be hidden – while <nav> elements non directly children of the slide (i.e., sub-children) will be normally displayed.
    2. The first <header> element found to be direct child of the slide will be placed on the top side of the viewport. Other <header> direct children will be normally displayed within the flow body of the main parent (i.e., on the right side, see below).
    3. The first <footer> element found to be direct child of the slide will be placed on the bottom-right corner of the viewport. Other <footer> direct children will be displayed normally within the flow body of the main parent (i.e., on the right side, see below).
    4. The first <aside> element found to be direct child of the slide will be placed on the bottom-left corner of the viewport. Other <aside> direct children will be displayed normally within the flow body of the main parent (i.e., on the right side, see below)..
    5. The first direct child found to be neither <nav>, nor <header>, nor <footer>, nor <aside> – i.e., CSS syntax: :not(nav):not(header):not(footer):not(aside) – will be placed at the center of the slide and will be considered the main content of the slide. From now on, this will be called “The Fifth Element” or “the <leeloo> element” (after Luc Besson's movie).
    6. Further direct children non matching the previous conditions will be displayed on the right side of the screen, in their normal order; the overflow property of this portion of the slide is set on hidden, so if you want a scrollable content you must create a scrollable container.
  • Non-direct children (i.e., sub-children) of the viewport:
    1. <a href="…"> elements direct children of the viewport's main <nav> will be considered as internal navigation links, and displayed on the left side of the viewport (with the exception of a[rel="up"]). Within the main <nav> container, Take five! is sensitive to the rel attribute. In particular:
      1. article.slide > nav:first-of-type a[rel="index"] will be accompained by the label “Index”
      2. article.slide > nav:first-of-type a[rel="first"] will be accompained by the label “First slide”
      3. article.slide > nav:first-of-type a[rel="next"] will be accompained by the label “Next slide”
      4. article.slide > nav:first-of-type a[rel="prev"] will be accompained by the label “Previous slide”
      5. article.slide > nav:first-of-type a[rel="last"] will be accompained by the label “Last slide”
      6. article.slide > nav:first-of-type a[rel="bookmark"] will be accompained by the label “Share it!”
      7. article.slide > nav:first-of-type a[rel="author"] will be accompained by the label “Author”
      8. article.slide > nav:first-of-type a[rel="up"], will be accompained by the label “Back to” and showed in background
      9. leeloo a[rel="up"]:empty will be accompained by the label “Click to exit” and showed in background
      Custom labels can replace the default labels by setting a data-label attribute on the link. Furthermore, it is possible to remove the default labels by assigning the "no-label" class – as in: <a href="#sample-photo" rel="next" class="no-label">Sample photo</a>.
    2. Each of the elements matching the previous selectors can be omitted (but either #8 or #9 should not be omitted); when present, it is very important that each of them occur only once for each slide. Additional duplications of the above cited a[rel="..."] elements, but placed elsewhere (e.g.: article.slide > div a[rel="up"]), are not relevant and can exist. All the previous 9 elements (note: also elements #8 and #9) can cohabitate together.
    3. All other sub-children will be displayed normally.

Issues

For compatibility issues with other CSS, here follows the table of all the z-indices assigned by Take five!. They cover the range 11110-11118, therefore no other elements within the document should have a z-index higher than 11110 in order to not be displayed over the viewport.

Targetz-index
article.slide > header:first-of-type 11110
article.slide > nav a[rel="up"] 11111
article.slide > aside:first-of-type 11112
article.slide > footer:first-of-type 11113
article.slide > *:not(nav:first-of-type,header:first-of-type,footer:first-of-type,aside:first-of-type,leeloo) 11114
leeloo 11115
article.slide > nav a:not([rel="up"]) 11116
article.slide > nav a[rel="up"]::after 11117
leeloo a[rel="up"]:empty::before 11118

Notes

  • For learning more about the itemscope attribute and HTML5 microdata, please visit schema.org.
  • It is possible to prevent the hashtag from being displayed in the viewport by using the class "no-hashtag" – as in <article itemscope class="slide no-hashtag" id="my-hash-name"> … </article>.
  • The special link a[rel="up"] complies with the following rule:
    • If it is inside a <nav> element ⇒ it will be shown on the top-right corner; a transparent, clickable, fixed, full-screen layer will be created.
    • If it is inside the <leeloo> element and is empty ⇒ a transparent clickable layer will cover the whole (and only) <leeloo> element.
    • If, instead,
      • it is inside the <leeloo> element but is not empty,
      • or it is outside both the <nav> and the <leeloo> elements (empty or not empty),
      • or if a[rel="up"] *is* the <leeloo> element iself – very unlikely...
      then ⇒ it will be ignored by this CSS and will be normally displayed.
